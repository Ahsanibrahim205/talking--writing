Why and how talking &
writing can improve life

  • Research has shown that by expressing our thoughts and feelings we can improve our general sense of well-being and help reduce unwanted tensions.
  • Have a conversation with someone close to you, and / or you trust, about some of your current thoughts and feelings. If this is too threatening, or inappropriate, then sometimes a complete, or relative, stranger may be an appropriate listener.
  • Write down your thoughts and feelings on a piece of paper either to keep, or throw away.
  • You can make a little ritual out of this of discarding these pieces of paper such as: with your eyes closed, throwing them purposefully over your shoulder, screwing them into really tight balls and then throwing them hard into a bin.
  • Drawing a picture to represent your feelings and/or the situation.
  • Modelling them in clay or plasticine.
